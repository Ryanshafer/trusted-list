In Ryan's own words

What help do people ask you for?

"Usually it's design leadership — how to build a team, how to advocate for quality when the pressure is on, how to make decisions without all the information. Sometimes it's more specific: portfolio feedback, navigating a tricky team dynamic, or thinking through a career pivot. I try to be the kind of advisor I wish I'd had earlier."

What problems do you love solving?

"Anything at the intersection of systems and people. I love the moment when a messy, tangled problem starts to reveal its underlying structure. Design systems are an obvious version of that, but so is org design, or figuring out why a product keeps confusing people in the same spot. The common thread is: there's usually a hidden logic waiting to be found."

What does a great work day look like?

"A morning of deep focus — no meetings, just me and a complex problem. A mid-day conversation that challenges how I've been thinking about it. Enough context-switching to feel useful, but not so much that I can't get back into flow. Finishing with something that didn't exist in the morning. And preferably outside somewhere in the afternoon."
